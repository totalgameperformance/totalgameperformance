The Program

Six Weeks.
One System.

The Pressure Lab is a structured, cohort-based program. You're not going it alone — you're going through it with 6–10 athletes who are facing the same mental battles you are. That shared pressure is part of the training.

Each week builds on the last. By Week 6, you're not just finishing a program — you're walking away with a fully custom mental performance system that belongs to you for the rest of your career.

W1
Identity & Baseline
Understand your current mental patterns. Identify your pressure triggers. Set individual targets for the 6 weeks ahead. Personal video review of your baseline assessment from Dan.
W2
Focus Control
Learn the attentional system and how pressure hijacks it. Build your personal focus anchor. Live drill during the call so you can feel it working immediately.
W3
Emotional Regulation
The missing piece most athletes never train. Pressure simulation exercises, group debrief, and a reset protocol you can run in 45 seconds between plays.

Confidence Under Fire
Confidence as a system, not a feeling. Case studies from elite performers, a personal challenge assignment, and a pre-competition mental routine.
W6
Integration & Game-Day Protocol
Each athlete presents their personal mental performance system to the group. This is yours forever. The game-day protocol that goes into every competition from here on out.

Common Questions

Answered Honestly

I've never done mental coaching before. Will I be behind?
No — the program is designed for athletes starting from zero. Week 1 is a baseline assessment and personal video review so we know exactly where you are before anything else happens. Every athlete in the cohort starts at their own baseline. The group dynamic actually accelerates learning because you're hearing how other athletes approach the same challenges.
What if I miss a call?
Every session is recorded and kept accessible for the duration of the program (Bonus 3). Missing a call isn't ideal — live participation is where a lot of the value happens — but it won't derail your progress. Just watch the recording and complete the week's assignments before the next call.
How much time does this actually take per week?
One 60-minute call, plus 10 minutes a day. That's it. The daily mental reps are designed to be short and high-frequency because that's how mental habits are actually built — not through hour-long sessions, but through consistent daily practice. Total weekly commitment: roughly 1.5–2 hours.
Is this only for soccer players?
The framework is built through a soccer lens — the examples, the pressure scenarios, and the language are all sport-specific. That said, the mental performance principles apply across all competitive sports. If you play a different sport and the content resonates, reach out and we'll talk about fit.
What happens after the 6 weeks?
You walk away with a complete personal mental performance system — your game-day protocol, your reset tools, your self-talk framework. Everything is yours to keep. For athletes who want to maintain and deepen the practice, the TGP Membership is available as a monthly option. But it's not required — the 6-week program is designed to be complete on its own.
Why only 10 athletes per cohort?
Because the group dynamic only works when it's small enough to be honest. Ten people is the maximum where every athlete can still get personal attention, participate in hot-seat coaching, and build real accountability relationships with their cohort. When it gets bigger than that, it becomes a webinar. That's not what this is.
